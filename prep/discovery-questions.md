# Discovery Questions — Daily Basket (09:30–10:30)

> Goal of the hour: pin the **one decision**, the **system of record**, the **write boundary**, and a **€ success number**. Get one real example of each claim type. Lead with restate-then-solve.

## Open by restating (earns trust, catches misframing)

> "So the real job isn't digitizing Jenny's sheet — it's recovering money suppliers owe you that's slipping through because the process lives in one place and some of it never gets logged. The risky part is claiming the *right* amount you can defend to the supplier. Is that the right framing?"

## Jenny Walsh — the operator (process truth)

- Walk me through **one claim end to end** today: what triggers it, where you look, what you type, who you email, when it's "done."
- How do you currently **spot** a claim — do you check every PO, or only when something looks off?
- Which claim types do you **actually log vs. let slide**? (short / damage / price-gap / rebate / promo)
- How do you know a **rebate or promo threshold** was hit — who tells you, what do you check?
- What do the **tracker statuses** mean, and when does money actually come back?
- What **breaks when you're on leave**?
- Show me a claim a supplier **disputed or rejected** — why did it fail?
- **Units:** where do per-case vs. per-unit issues bite you (beyond dairy/SUP-003)?

## Paula Hart — the sponsor (outcome & boundary)

- If this works, what does success look like in **90 days** — what number do you report up to Mark?
- Do you have a current estimate of **claim leakage / recovery rate**?
- What's the **process boundary** — claim *identification* only, or also *submission* and *cash collection*?
- Who has **authority to approve/submit** a claim? What should the human gate be?
- Is there a **threshold** below which you don't bother claiming?
- Appetite: **identify-and-recommend** vs. **auto-submit**?

## Mark Bryant — the economic buyer (the number that moves)

- What **€ figure** would make this a clear win for you?
- Is it **recovered cash**, or also **Jenny's time / key-person risk**, or both?
- How do you want this **reported** (one number, a dashboard, a monthly claim pack)?

## Access & systems (unblocks the build)

- Can the agent **read the ERP exports directly**, or only the spreadsheet?
- Is there a real **claim-submission** endpoint (portal / email / AP system), or is the tracker the end of the line?
- **Sandbox vs. prod** — what writes am I allowed to make on the day?
- Can I get a **real example record** for each claim type, and the contract for one supplier?

## The three things to leave the hour with

1. **System of record** confirmed = ERP exports + contracts (not the sheet).
2. **Write boundary** confirmed = identify + claim-pack + human approval (MVP), submission stubbed.
3. **€ success criterion** agreed in writing before any code.
