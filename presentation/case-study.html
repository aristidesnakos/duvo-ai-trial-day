  1. Problem

    Suppliers owe Daily Basket money all the time — short and damaged deliveries, prices above the agreed list, rebates and promo earned but never claimed. For a ~€180M online grocer on thin margins, that adds up. But it all lived in one spreadsheet, kept by one person (Jenny), so money nobody logged was money nobody recovered.

  2. Symptoms

    When Mark, the Finance Director, asked "how much are we recovering vs. how much are we owed?", Finance had no answer. Claims were caught by hand ("I catch what I catch"), rebate thresholds went unchecked, and the two weeks Jenny was out sick the process stopped — with credit windows about to close.

  3. Approach — shipped, not prototyped

    Rather than digitize the spreadsheet, the agent sorts every claim into missed / logged-correct / over-claimed / not-claimable and hands off a human-gated pack. Live on Duvo as a two-agent closed loop over a Sheets surrogate ERP — the supplier side answers from its own ledger, so credits and disputes are data-driven, not scripted. It runs whether or not Jenny is in.

  4. Proof — before / after

    Before: € owed unknown, recovery unanswerable. After: €6,203 owed · €4,628 never logged · €450 duplicate caught · €29,200 double-billing blocked → €35,403 protected. Recovered to date: €0 — claims are identified, not yet collected; the recovery loop was demonstrated end-to-end on Duvo.

  5. Trustworthy

    It won't invent money. The dairy claim Jenny could never get to balance comes out to €0 once the per-case units are converted — so they can stop chasing it. A shortage with no goods receipt is left unclaimable rather than guessed, and only the one supplier that genuinely crossed its rebate threshold is claimed. It's as clear about where the money isn't as where it is.
